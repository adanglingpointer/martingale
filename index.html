The Martingale is a strategy of betting that relies on a very simple premise: every time you lose, you double down on the next round.

Theoretically, if you have infinite money to wager, you will be guaranteed to win on the nth bet by doubling down each time.

Unfortunately, money is limited, and casinos put betting limits on games. However, this doesn't mean that the Martingale method can not still be applied--by betting in a favorable position every time.

In the case of roulette, one can simply wait for a bet to come out thrice before betting on the opposite. For example, if black hits three times in a row, then we can bet the minimum bet on red for the fourth spin. This provides us the statistical benefit of the Martingale method while reducing risk of loss.
